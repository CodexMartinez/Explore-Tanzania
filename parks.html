Discover Tanzania's National Parks

Home to some of Africa’s most breathtaking landscapes and wildlife

Our Top National Parks

Serengeti National Park

Experience the great wildebeest migration, vast savannahs, and big cats in their natural habitat. The park is also home to over 500 species of birds, making it a paradise for bird watchers. Key features: Annual migration, Big Five, hot air balloon safaris, luxury lodges.

Ngorongoro Crater

Visit a collapsed volcano teeming with wildlife and offering dramatic views and safaris. It is one of the best places in Tanzania to spot the endangered black rhino. Highlights: World Heritage Site, Maasai communities, dense animal population, scenic caldera.

Mikumi National Park

Close to Dar es Salaam, Mikumi offers easy access to elephants, giraffes, and hippos in the wild. The park's open horizons make it ideal for photography and game drives. Attractions: Mkata floodplain, guided safaris, picnic sites, affordable lodges.

Ruaha National Park

One of Tanzania’s largest parks, Ruaha is home to lions, elephants, and birdlife in vast wilderness. It offers a raw safari experience with fewer tourists. Features: Great Ruaha River, baobab trees, lion prides, walking safaris.

Selous Game Reserve

Known for its river safaris and boat trips, Selous is one of Africa’s largest protected areas, with a rich variety of wildlife including crocodiles, hippos, and wild dogs. Specialties: Rufiji River boating, fly camping, unique ecosystems.

Katavi National Park

A remote and untouched park offering thrilling sightings of buffalo, lions, and massive pods of hippos. Perfect for adventurous travelers seeking solitude in nature. Notable sights: Katuma River, massive buffalo herds, untouched wilderness.

Udzungwa Mountains National Park

Famous for its rainforest and hiking trails, Udzungwa is ideal for trekking and discovering endemic plant and animal species found nowhere else. Activities: Sanje waterfalls trek, endemic monkey species, biodiversity exploration.
